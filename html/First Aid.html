3.Bleeding:

               First Aid Treatment:

               1. Stop Bleeding byapplying direct pressure on the cut or wound with a clean cloth, tissue,or piece of gauze until bleeding stops.

               2.If blood soaks through the material, don’t remove it.Put more cloth or gauze on top of it and continue to apply pressure.

               3.If the wound is on the arm or leg, raise limb above the heart, if possible, to help slow bleeding. Wash your hands again after giving first aid and before cleaning and dressing the wound.

               4.Clean Cut or Wound and Gently clean with soap and warm water. Try to rinse soap out of wound to prevent irritation. Don’t use hydrogen peroxide or iodine, which can damage tissue.

               5.Do not apply a tourniquet unless the bleeding is severe and not stopped with direct pressure.Give them constant reassurance while waiting for the ambulance.

               6.Protect the Wound by applying antibiotic cream to reduce risk of infection and cover with a sterile bandage. Change the bandage daily to keep the wound clean and dry.

               7.Give them constant reassurance while waiting Ambulance and call an Ambulance Immediately on these numbers:-108/102/112.

              

5.Burns:

               First Aid Treatment:

               1.Smother any flames by covering them with a blanket or water.If your clothing catches fire,do not run: stop, drop, and roll on the ground to smother the flames.

               2.Cold temperature burns:Try first aid measures to warm the areas.Small areas of your body(ears,face,nose,fingers,toes) that are really cold or frozen can be warmed by blowing warm air on them, tucking them inside your clothing or putting them in warm water.

               3.Liquid scald burns (thermal burns): Run cool tap water over the burn for 10 to 20 minutes.Do not use ice.

               4.Remove any jewelry or clothing at the site of the burn.If clothing is stuck to the burn,do not remove it.Carefully cut around the stuck fabric to remove loose fabric.Remove all jewelry,because it may be hard to remove it later if swelling occurs.

               5.Cover the burn with a clean, dry cloth to reduce the risk of infection and do not put ice or butter on the burned area, because these measures do not help and can damage the skin tissue.

               6.Do not put any salve or medicine on the burned area,so your doctor can properly assess your burn.

               7.Give them constant reassurance while waiting Ambulance and call an Ambulance Immediately on these numbers:-108/102/112.

6.Choking:

               First Aid Treatment:

               If the Person Is Conscious but Not Able to Breathe or Talk:

               1.Give up to 5 blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.

               2.If the person is not pregnant or too obese, do abdominal thrusts.

               3.Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around the waist.

               4.Place your clenched fist just above the person’s navel. Grab your fist with your other hand. Quickly pull inward and upward as if trying to lift the person up

               5.If the blockage is still not dislodged, continue cycles of 5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts until the object is coughed up or the person starts to breathe or cough.

               6.Take the object out of his mouth only if you can see it. Never do a finger sweep unless you can see the object in the person's mouth.

               7.Give them constant reassurance while waiting Ambulance and call an Ambulance Immediately on these numbers:-108/102/112.

10.Heat Stroke

               1.Heat stroke can kill or cause damage to the brain and other internal organs.Although heat stroke mainly affects people over age 50, it also takes a toll on healthy young athletes.

               2.Heat stroke often occurs as a progression from milder heat-related illnesses such as heat cramps,heat syncope (fainting),and heat exhaustion, but it can strike even if you have no previous signs of heat injury.

Symptoms of Heat Stroke

               The hallmark symptom of heat stroke is a core body temperature above 104 degrees Fahrenheit,but fainting may be the first sign.

               Other symptoms may include:

               1.Throbbing headache

               2.Dizziness and light-headedness

               3.Lack of sweating despite the heat

               4.Red, hot, and dry skin

               5.Muscle weakness or cramps

               6.Nausea and vomiting

               7.Rapid heartbeat, which may be either strong or weak

               8.Rapid, shallow breathing

               9.ehavioral changes such as confusion, disorientation, or staggering

               10.Seizuresand Unconsciousness

               First Aid Treatment:

               1.If you suspect that someone has a heat stroke, immediately call 108/102/112 or transport the person to a hospital. Any delay seeking medical help can be fatal.

               2.While waiting for the paramedics to arrive, initiate first aid. Move the person to an air-conditioned environment -- or at least a cool, shady area -- and remove any unnecessary clothing.

               3.If possible, take the person's core body temperature and initiate first aid to cool it to 101 to 102 degrees Fahrenheit. Fan air over the patient while wetting his or her skin with water from a sponge or garden hose. Apply ice packs to the patient's armpits, groin, neck, and back.Because these areas are rich with blood vessels close to the skin, cooling them may reduce body temperature.

               4.Immerse the patient in a shower or tub of cool water. If the person is young and heathy and suffered heat stroke while exercising vigorously -- what’s known as exertional heat stroke -- you can use an ice bath to help cool the body.

               5.Do not use ice for older patients, young children, patients with chronic illness, or anyone whose heat stroke occurred without vigorous exercise. Doing so can be dangerous.

               6.If emergency response is delayed, call the hospital emergency room for additional instructions.

               7.Medications. These include antihistamines, diet pills, diuretics, sedatives, tranquilizers, stimulants, seizure medications(anticonvulsants), heart and blood pressure medications such as beta-blockers and vasoconstrictors,and medications for psychiatric illnesses such as antidepressants and antipsychotics.Illegal drugs such as cocaine and methamphetamine also are associated with increased risk of heat stroke.

               8.Give them constant reassurance while waiting Ambulance and call an Ambulance Immediately on these numbers:-108/102/112.

               Precaution:

               1.Monitoring the color of your urine. arker urine is a sign of dehydration.Be sure to drink enough fluids to maintain very light-colored urine. Measuring your weight before and after physical activity.

               2.Monitoring lost water weight can help you determine how much fluid you need to drink.Avoid fluids containing caffeine or alcohol,because both substances can make you lose more fluids and worsen heat-related illness.

               3.Do not take salt tablets unless your doctor has told you to do so. The easiest and safest way to replace salt and other electrolytes during heat waves is to drink sports beverages or fruit juice.

               4.Check with your doctor before increasing liquid intake if you have epilepsy or heart, kidney, or liver disease; are on fluid-restricted diets; or have a problem with fluid retention.
